## Chapter 1 — Modules 1–3

### Data availability  
The degree or extent to which timely and relevant information is readily accessible and able to be put to use.  
**Tags:** data-quality, accessibility  

### Data integrity  
The accuracy, completeness, consistency, and trustworthiness of data throughout its life cycle.  
**Tags:** data-quality, lifecycle  

### Data visibility  
The degree or extent to which information can be identified, monitored, and integrated from disparate internal and external sources.  
**Tags:** data-management, visibility  

### Vanity metric  
Data points intended to impress others but not indicative of actual performance, thus offering no meaningful business insight.  
**Tags:** analytics, metrics  

---

### A  

**Application programming interface (API)**  
A set of functions and procedures that integrate computer programs, forming a connection that enables them to communicate.  
**Tags:** integration, development  

**Applications software developer**  
A person who designs computer or mobile applications, generally for consumers.  
**Tags:** career, development  

---

### B  

**Business intelligence (BI)**  
Automating processes and information channels to transform relevant data into actionable insights that are easily available to decision-makers.  
**Tags:** business-intelligence, analytics  

**Business intelligence governance**  
A process for defining and implementing business intelligence systems and frameworks within an organization.  
**Tags:** governance, strategy  

**Business intelligence monitoring**  
Building and using hardware and software tools to easily and rapidly analyze data and enable stakeholders to make impactful business decisions.  
**Tags:** monitoring, bi-tools  

**Business intelligence stages**  
The sequence of stages that determine BI business value and organizational data maturity — capture, analyze, and monitor.  
**Tags:** lifecycle, bi-process  

**Business intelligence strategy**  
The management of people, processes, and tools used in the BI process.  
**Tags:** bi-strategy  

---

### D  

**Data analysts**  
People who collect, transform, and organize data.  
**Tags:** career, analytics  

**Data governance professionals**  
People responsible for the formal management of an organization’s data assets.  
**Tags:** governance  

**Data maturity**  
The extent to which an organization can effectively use its data to extract actionable insights.  
**Tags:** data-management  

**Data model**  
A tool for organizing data elements and how they relate to one another.  
**Tags:** modeling  

**Data pipeline**  
A series of processes that transport data from different sources to their final destination for storage and analysis.  
**Tags:** etl, data-flow  

**Data warehousing specialists**  
People who develop processes and procedures to effectively store and organize data.  
**Tags:** warehousing, career  

**Deliverable**  
Any product, service, or result that must be achieved in order to complete a project.  
**Tags:** project-management  

**Developer**  
A person who uses programming languages to create, execute, test, and troubleshoot software applications.  
**Tags:** career, development  

---

### E  

**ETL (Extract, Transform, Load)**  
A type of data pipeline that gathers data from source systems, converts it into a useful format, and loads it into a data warehouse or other destination system.  
**Tags:** etl, pipelines  

---

### I  

**Information technology professionals**  
People who test, install, repair, upgrade, and maintain hardware and software solutions.  
**Tags:** IT, career  

**Iteration**  
Repeating a procedure over and over again to approach the desired result.  
**Tags:** development, testing  

---

### K  

**Key performance indicator (KPI)**  
A quantifiable value closely linked to business strategy, used to track progress toward a goal.  
**Tags:** metrics, analytics  

---

### M  

**Metric**  
A single, quantifiable data point used to evaluate performance.  
**Tags:** analytics  

---

### P  

**Portfolio**  
A collection of materials that can be shared with potential employers.  
**Tags:** career  

**Project manager**  
Person who handles a project’s day-to-day steps, scope, schedule, budget, and resources.  
**Tags:** management, project  

**Project sponsor**  
Person with overall accountability for a project and who establishes its success criteria.  
**Tags:** stakeholder, management  

---

### S  

**Strategy**  
A plan for achieving a goal or arriving at a desired future state.  
**Tags:** planning, management  

**Systems analyst**  
Person who identifies ways to design, implement, and advance information systems to meet business goals.  
**Tags:** IT, systems  

**Systems software developer**  
Developer who builds applications and programs for backend processing systems.  
**Tags:** development  

---

### T  

**Tactic**  
A method used to enable an accomplishment.  
**Tags:** strategy, planning

## Chapter 2 — Module 1  

**Attribute**  
In a dimensional model, a characteristic or quality used to describe a dimension.  
**Tags:** dimensional-modeling  

**Columnar database**  
A database organized by columns instead of rows.  
**Tags:** storage, database  

**Combined systems**  
Database systems that store and analyze data in the same location.  
**Tags:** hybrid-database  

**Compiled programming language**  
A language that compiles coded instructions executed directly by the target machine.  
**Tags:** programming  

**Data lake**  
A system that stores large amounts of raw data in its original format until needed.  
**Tags:** storage, raw-data  

**Data mart**  
A subject-oriented database subset of a larger data warehouse.  
**Tags:** warehouse, BI  

**Data warehouse**  
A specific type of database that consolidates data from multiple source systems for consistent and efficient access.  
**Tags:** warehouse, data-management  

**Database migration**  
Moving data from one source platform to another target database.  
**Tags:** migration, data-engineering  

**Dimension (data modeling)**  
A piece of information providing detail and context to a fact.  
**Tags:** dimensional-modeling  

**Dimension table**  
The table storing the attributes of the dimensions of a fact.  
**Tags:** schema, relational-model  

**Design pattern**  
A solution template using relevant measures and facts to create a model supporting business needs.  
**Tags:** modeling, schema-design  

**Dimensional model**  
A relational model optimized for rapid retrieval from a data warehouse.  
**Tags:** BI, modeling  

**Distributed database**  
A database distributed across multiple physical locations.  
**Tags:** distributed-systems  

**Fact**  
A measurement or metric in a dimensional model.  
**Tags:** BI, metrics  

**Fact table**  
A table containing measurements or metrics related to an event.  
**Tags:** schema  

**Foreign key**  
A field in a table that is a primary key in another table.  
**Tags:** relational-db  

**Functional programming language**  
A programming paradigm centered on functions.  
**Tags:** programming  

**Google DataFlow**  
A serverless data-processing service that reads data from the source, transforms it, and writes it to a destination.  
**Tags:** GCP, ETL  

**Interpreted programming language**  
A programming language that uses an interpreter to execute instructions.  
**Tags:** programming  

**Logical data modeling**  
Representing tables and their relationships in a physical data model.  
**Tags:** modeling  

**Object-oriented programming language**  
Programming language modeled around data objects.  
**Tags:** programming  

**OLAP (Online Analytical Processing) system**  
A system optimized for analysis and aggregation across databases.  
**Tags:** analytics, BI  

**OLTP (Online Transaction Processing) database**  
A database optimized for transaction processing, not analysis.  
**Tags:** database, operations  

**Primary key**  
A unique identifier for each record in a table.  
**Tags:** keys, integrity  

**Python**  
A general-purpose programming language.  
**Tags:** programming, analytics  

**Response time**  
The time it takes for a database to complete a user request.  
**Tags:** performance  

**Row-based database**  
A database organized by rows.  
**Tags:** storage, OLTP  

**Separated storage and computing systems**  
Databases where data is stored remotely and analyzed locally.  
**Tags:** architecture  

**Single-homed database**  
All data stored in a single physical location.  
**Tags:** legacy, database  

**Snowflake schema**  
An extension of a star schema with additional dimensions and subdimensions.  
**Tags:** schema  

**Star schema**  
A schema consisting of one fact table referencing multiple dimension tables.  
**Tags:** schema  

**Target table**  
The predetermined destination where pipeline data is sent for action.  
**Tags:** ETL, pipelines

## Chapter 2 — Module 2  

**Contention**  
When two or more components attempt to use a single resource simultaneously in a conflicting way.  
**Tags:** database, performance  

**Data partitioning**  
Dividing a database into logical parts to improve query processing and manageability.  
**Tags:** performance, partitioning  

**Database performance**  
A measure of workload capacity and cost efficiency.  
**Tags:** optimization  

**ELT (Extract, Load, Transform)**  
A pipeline approach where data is loaded before being transformed.  
**Tags:** ETL, pipelines  

**Fragmented data**  
Data broken into multiple pieces not stored together due to frequent use or modifications.  
**Tags:** data-management  

**Index**  
An organizational tag used to quickly locate data within a system.  
**Tags:** optimization  

**Optimization**  
Maximizing speed and efficiency of data retrieval.  
**Tags:** tuning, performance  

**Query plan**  
The set of steps a database takes to execute a query.  
**Tags:** SQL, execution  

**Resources**  
Hardware and software tools available to a database system.  
**Tags:** infrastructure  

**Subject-oriented**  
Associated with a specific area or department of a business.  
**Tags:** BI, organization  

**Throughput**  
The overall capability of a database’s hardware and software to process requests.  
**Tags:** performance  

**Workload**  
Combination of transactions, queries, and commands processed by the database at any time.  
**Tags:** performance

## Chapter 2 — Module 3  

**Accuracy**  
A validation element ensuring data reflects the actual entity being measured.  
**Tags:** data-quality  

**Business rule**  
A statement defining a restriction on specific database elements.  
**Tags:** governance  

**Completeness**  
Validation ensuring all necessary data components are present.  
**Tags:** data-quality  

**Conformity**  
Validation ensuring data matches the required destination format.  
**Tags:** data-quality  

**Consistency**  
Validation ensuring data aligns and agrees across systems.  
**Tags:** data-quality  

**Data dictionary**  
A collection describing content, format, and structure of data objects.  
**Tags:** metadata  

**Data lineage**  
Identifying the origin, movement, and transformation history of data.  
**Tags:** metadata, governance  

**Data mapping**  
Matching fields from one data source to another.  
**Tags:** ETL, integration  

**Integrity**  
Ensuring accuracy, completeness, and trustworthiness throughout the data lifecycle.  
**Tags:** quality  

**Quality testing**  
Checking data for defects using seven validation elements: completeness, consistency, conformity, accuracy, redundancy, integrity, and timeliness.  
**Tags:** validation  

**Redundancy**  
Validation confirming no more data than necessary is processed.  
**Tags:** optimization, quality  

**Schema validation**  
Ensures the source schema matches the target database schema.  
**Tags:** validation  

**Timeliness**  
Validation confirming data is current.  
**Tags:** data-quality

## Chapter 3 — Module 1  

**Audience problem**  
A dashboard issue caused by failing to consider user needs.  
**Tags:** visualization, design  

**Data problem**  
A dashboard issue caused by poor or incorrect data sources.  
**Tags:** visualization, quality  

**Low-fidelity mockup**  
A simple draft of a visualization for planning and feedback.  
**Tags:** visualization, design  

**Tool problem**  
A dashboard issue related to hardware or software limitations.  
**Tags:** visualization, tools

## Chapter 3 — Module 2  

**Dimension (visualization)**  
A qualitative data type used to categorize data.  
**Tags:** visualization, data-type  

**Encoding**  
Translating dimensions and measures into visual representations.  
**Tags:** visualization  

**Measure**  
A quantitative data type that can be discrete or continuous.  
**Tags:** visualization  

**Object-level permission**  
A privacy setting controlling the availability of a single item.  
**Tags:** permissions, security  

**Pre-aggregation**  
Performing calculations on data while it remains in the database.  
**Tags:** performance, optimization  

**Processing speed**  
How quickly a program updates and loads data.  
**Tags:** performance  

**Public availability**  
A privacy setting allowing anyone to access a dashboard.  
**Tags:** permissions  

**Row-level permission**  
A privacy setting controlling access to specific rows in a dataset.  
**Tags:** permissions, security  

**Trade-off**  
Balancing various factors by prioritizing one element over another to reach the best result.  
**Tags:** optimization, decision-making
